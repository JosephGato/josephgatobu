A Memoir: Must've Been Fate

It all began with numbers. Mathematics—it was as if the world itself had written its laws in the language of equations, and I was simply trying to decipher them. But back then, I knew nothing more than the fact that I must attend school, where the classroom was a world within itself, detached from the happenings outside. I didn't care much about the noise beyond those walls.

In time, math became more than just a subject. It evolved into something fascinating, a foundation that, when paired with physics, chemistry, and biology, created a world of pure logic and discovery. It was a puzzle—a universe where everything connected, where numbers explained the unexplained, and I was in awe.

Then, slowly, my gaze shifted from numbers to something new, something I hadn’t anticipated—computers. I was drawn in by the allure of technology, the sleek gadgets, the hum of machines, the promise of possibilities. Music was my first love in the tech world. I marveled at how seamlessly devices could blend art with engineering, turning sound into waves, and waves into something that moved souls.

From there, tech became my thing—a growing passion that pulled me deeper. With each discovery, each piece of hardware, each line of code, I felt the shift within me. IT wasn’t just a hobby anymore; it was becoming a part of my identity.

And then came something even more powerful—data. The way it flowed, the way it revealed patterns, how it could shape the future. I was hooked. Information, knowledge, wisdom—they all seemed like keys to unlocking some grand secret, a mystery that I was determined to unravel. Grace, it seemed, had led me here.

Years later, I found myself pursuing a degree in Data Science, Computer Technology, and Networking. Each subject was a revelation, each challenge a thrill. It was more than education; it was a revolution in my mind. Technology was no longer just a tool—it was a bridge to endless possibilities, a world where solutions met problems, where creativity met logic. It became clear: this was my calling.

Today, I stand proud as a competent Information Technologist, armed with a Bachelor of Science in Information Technology from Jomo Kenyatta University of Agriculture and Technology. I walked away with more than just a degree—I left with a vision and a passion for what lies ahead.

My journey has equipped me with a rich toolkit—skills in Graphic Design, Software Programming, and development, and a deep understanding of how to thrive in a Client/Server Computer Environment. But the story doesn’t end here.

The future beckons, and with it, a call to direct my proven technical systems technology skills towards projects that push the boundaries of what’s possible. There’s a hunger in me—a drive to take theory and practice, to merge creativity with logic, and to build systems that make a difference.

It’s been quite the journey—from math to machines, from curiosity to competence. Must’ve been fate that led me here.
